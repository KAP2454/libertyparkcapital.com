# Website Simplification Plan: Austin Asset Manager Landing Page

## Current Website Analysis

### Structure Overview
The current General Intelligence Company website features:
1. **Hero Section** - Large hero image with company name and tagline
2. **Vision Statement Section** - Multi-paragraph vision statement with animated text
3. **Coordinator/Technical Section** - Complex interactive diagram showing isolated systems
4. **Product Section** - Large Cofounder product promotion with blue background
5. **Blog Section** - Featured articles with images
6. **Footer** - Standard footer with copyright and social links

### Navigation
- Home, About, Writing, Careers (4 pages)

### Key Complexity Elements
- Complex scroll animations and interactions
- Multiple product/service sections
- Interactive technical diagrams
- Blog/article content
- Multiple CTAs throughout
- Animated text reveals
- Sticky positioning elements

## Simplified Landing Page Plan for Austin Asset Manager

### Core Objective
Create a clean, professional, trustworthy landing page that establishes credibility and provides clear information about the asset management firm.

### Target Audience
- High-net-worth individuals
- Institutional investors
- Potential clients seeking asset management services

### Simplified Structure

#### 1. **Hero Section** (Above the fold)
- **Remove:** Large background  animations, complex text animations, time/date displays
- **Keep:** Clean hero layout, company name
- **New Content:**
  - Company name: "Liberty Park Capital Management"
  - Tagline: Professional, concise value proposition (e.g., "Preserving and growing wealth for clients in Austin and beyond")
  - Location: "Austin, Texas" prominently displayed
  - Single CTA: "Learn More"
  - Minimal, professional background (subtle pattern or solid color)

#### 2. **About/Philosophy Section**
- **Remove:** Complex technical diagrams, AI/agent terminology
- **New Content:**
  - Brief firm history (founded year, Austin-based)
  - Investment philosophy (3-4 key principles)
  - Team overview (founders/key partners with photos)
  - Credentials/licenses displayed

#### 3. **Services Section**
- **Remove:** Product demos, technical explanations
- **New Content:**
    - Overview of Fund Strategies
    

#### 4. **Approach/Methodology Section**
- **Remove:** Complex interactive elements, technical jargon
- **New Content:**
  - Clear explanation of investment approach
  - Asset allocation strategy
  - Risk management philosophy
  - Client-first service model


#### 5. **Contact Section**
- **Remove:** Product signup forms, newsletter subscriptions
- **New Content:**
  - Contact form
  - Office address (Austin location)
  - Phone number
  - Email address
  - Office hours (optional)

#### 6. **Footer**
- **Simplified:**
  - Company name and copyright
  - Links: About, Services, Approach, Contact, Disclaimer
  - Social links (LinkedIn, if applicable)
  - Compliance/regulatory disclosures link

## Design Simplification Guidelines

### Typography
- **Remove:** Complex animated text reveals, staggered word animations
- **Keep:** Clean, professional font hierarchy
- **Use:** 2-3 font sizes maximum, consistent spacing

### Color Palette
- **Remove:** Bright blues, complex gradients, multiple color sections
- **Use:** Professional palette:
  - Primary: Deep navy or charcoal gray (#1a1a1a or #2c3e50)
  - Accent: Trust-building blue (#0056b3 or #0066cc) or professional green
  - Background: Clean white or very light gray (#fafafa)
  - Text: High contrast for readability

### Imagery
- **Remove:** Abstract illustrations, animated diagrams, product screenshots
- **Use:** 
  - Professional headshots of team members
  - Austin skyline or office building (subtle, not dominant)
  - Stock images of professional office settings (sparingly)
  - Charts/graphs only if directly relevant to services

### Interactions
- **Remove:**
  - Complex scroll animations
  - Sticky positioning tricks
  - Interactive diagrams
  - Text blur/reveal effects
  - Hover state complexities
- **Keep:**
  - Simple hover states on buttons
  - Smooth scroll (native or minimal)
  - Form interactions

### Layout
- **Remove:**
  - Full-screen sections with complex positioning
  - Overlapping elements
  - Sticky navigation changes
- **Use:**
  - Clean, centered layouts
  - Generous white space
  - Clear section breaks
  - Consistent padding/margins

## Content Simplification

### Text Reduction
- **Current:** Multiple paragraphs, complex explanations
- **Target:** 
  - Hero: 10-15 words
  - Section headings: 3-7 words
  - Body paragraphs: 2-3 sentences max
  - Total page length: Scroll 3-4x viewport height (vs. current 5-6x+)

### CTA Simplification
- **Current:** Multiple CTAs (Get Cofounder, Get to know us, Read more articles, etc.)
- **Target:** 
  - Primary: "Schedule Consultation" (appears 2-3 times)
  - Secondary: "Contact Us" (in navigation and footer)

### Navigation Simplification
- **Remove:** Writing, Careers, Blog
- **Keep:** 
  - Home (optional, just logo)
  - About
  - Services
  - Approach
  - Contact

---

## Technical Simplification

### Performance Improvements
- Remove heavy animations and interactive libraries
- Reduce image file sizes
- Eliminate complex scroll listeners
- Simplify JavaScript to vanilla or minimal framework usage

### Accessibility
- Ensure proper heading hierarchy
- Add alt text to all images
- Ensure keyboard navigation works
- Maintain proper color contrast ratios
- Use semantic HTML

### Mobile Responsiveness
- Simplify mobile layouts (current site likely has complex responsive breakpoints)
- Use standard breakpoints (mobile: 320px+, tablet: 768px+, desktop: 1024px+)
- Test all forms and interactions on mobile

## Key Principles for Simplification

1. **Trust Over Flashy:** Professional appearance builds trust more than animations
2. **Clarity Over Complexity:** Clear information is more valuable than clever interactions
3. **Performance Over Features:** Fast, simple site beats slow, feature-rich site
4. **Accessibility First:** Ensure all users can access information easily
5. **Mobile First:** Design for mobile, enhance for desktop
6. **Regulatory Compliance:** Ensure all required disclosures are present and clear

---

## Notes for Implementation

- Consider using a simple CMS (like Sanity, Contentful, or even Markdown) for easy content updates
- Use a minimal CSS framework (Tailwind CSS utilities or plain CSS) to avoid bloat
- Consider hosting on a simple static site host (Vercel, Netlify) for performance
- Ensure SEC compliance requirements are met for financial services websites
- May need legal review of all content and disclaimers before launch
- Consider adding privacy policy and terms of service pages
